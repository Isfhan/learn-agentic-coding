# Step 18 · Staying Current

> **⏱️ Time:** ongoing · **Prereq:** All previous steps (or at least 00–05)

Agentic coding moves weekly. This final step is about **never falling behind**.

---

## 🎯 What you'll learn

- A lightweight **daily / weekly / monthly** learning cadence.
- The 10 people/publications to follow.
- The 5 repositories to watch.
- How to become a *producer* of knowledge, not just a consumer.

---

## 1. The cadence

### 🗓 Daily (5 minutes)
- Skim **2 tweets** from a short "AI tool" Twitter/X list (build your own — see §3).
- Star **1 repo** you come across.

### 📆 Weekly (30 minutes)
- One issue of **Simon Willison's Weeknotes** + **Elvis Saravia's newsletter** + **Latent Space podcast** (pick 1 episode).
- Skim release notes of: **Cursor, Claude Code, OpenAI Agents SDK**. All three publish often.
- Try **one new MCP server** or **one new skill**.

### 🗓 Monthly (2 hours)
- Read **1 research paper** from the "Top papers this month" feed.
- Build **1 tiny thing** (an MCP server, a skill, a hook). Post it publicly.

### 🗓 Quarterly (half a day)
- Re-read Anthropic's *Building Effective Agents* — you'll notice new things each time.
- Audit your rules/skills/hooks/MCP servers. Remove what you don't use.
- Update your `AGENTS.md`.

## 7. Become a producer

The best way to learn is to teach. Try one:

- **Write a blog post** about any step you struggled with. "How I built my first MCP server" does well.
- **Publish a skill or MCP server** to GitHub. Tiny is fine. 3 stars is a win.
- **Do a 30-second video** showing a cool agent workflow. Post on X or LinkedIn.
- **Do a lunch-and-learn** at work. You'll internalize 10x more than passive learning.

> **The best engineers in AI tooling today were beginners 18 months ago who wrote in public.**

---

## 8. The meta-skill: learning to *unlearn*

This field moves so fast that specific tools you love today will be gone or changed in 6 months. The sturdy assets are:

- **Fundamentals** — how LLMs work (Step 01), context engineering (Step 13).
- **Patterns** — the ones in Step 17.
- **Habits** — evals (Step 14), AGENTS.md (Step 07), sessions hygiene.
- **Taste** — the ability to tell good output from bad, quickly.

Protect those. Replace specific tools as needed.
